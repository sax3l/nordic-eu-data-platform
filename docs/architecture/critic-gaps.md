# Adversarial Completeness Critique — Architecture Blueprint

**Reviewer role:** completeness critic. **Date:** 2026-06-24.
**Scope reviewed:** `arch_oss-combo-matrix.md`, `arch_fusion-confidence.md`, `arch_speed-parallelism.md`, `arch_adaptive-engine.md`, `arch_licensed-tools.md`, cross-checked against the five `country_*.md` and `verify_nordic.md`.
**Verdict:** The architecture is strong on the *mechanism* layer (bandit routing, AIMD, log-odds fusion, cheap-confirm-skip). It is weak where it matters most for the user's eight goals: (1) the country bulk-source reality documented in the `country_*` files is **not wired into** the arch design, so the "use every free source maximally" and "skip-the-scrape" goals are under-delivered; (2) several **single points of failure** sit on the critical path; (3) multiple **compliance landmines** in the country files directly contradict the arch files' "scrape everything" framing; (4) **LinkedIn and vehicle coverage** — two of the five required entity types — are named as goals but never actually designed.

Gaps are ordered by severity. Each has: the gap, why it breaks a stated goal, and a concrete fix.

---

## P0 — CRITICAL (breaks a core goal or is a single point of failure)

### P0-1. The free bulk registries — the single biggest speed + coverage lever — are not integrated, only name-dropped.
**Gap.** The `country_*` files prove that **most company-level fields for ~10 of 20 markets are available as free, WAF-free, whole-country BULK files**: SE Bolagsverket bulkfil + iXBRL weekly, NO brreg `/api/enheter/lastned` (+roller/totalbestand), DK CVR S2S Elasticsearch, FI PRH all-companies JSON, FR SIRENE/INPI SFTP (Parquet), BE KBO daily full + SFTP, NL KVK + RDW, UK Companies House + PSC + accounts, IE CRO, CZ ARES XML, PL KRS/REGON, CH Zefix/LINDAS SPARQL, AT/DE HVD/OffeneRegister, GLEIF golden copy. The arch files mention "Bolagsverket/brreg/PRH" only as a *tier-0 confirmer that races a scrape* (`arch_adaptive-engine §7`, `arch_oss-combo-matrix §0`). They never model a **bulk-ingest-first lane** that downloads the whole register once and answers 30–60% of all entities with **zero fetches**.
**Why it breaks goals.** Goals #3 (max speed), #7 (combine sources to raise speed), #8 (cover every entity). `arch_speed-parallelism §3a` even asserts "registry bulk-dumps already answer 30–60% ... the single largest speed win" — but no arch file actually builds the bulk-ingest pipeline, the `registry_bulk_index`, or the delta-feed subscriber it depends on. The biggest lever is described as a footnote, not architected.
**Fix.** Add a first-class **Bulk Ingestion Plane** as Tier -1 (cheaper than tier-0 confirm):
- A per-country `BulkSource` registry (URL, format, cadence, license, parser) seeded directly from the `country_*` files. Schedule each to its native cadence (brreg nightly, KBO/SIRENE daily, SE iXBRL weekly, CH Zefix daily, GLEIF daily).
- Load into the `registry_bulk_index` (and the Bloom filter) **before** any fetch lane runs; the dedup-before-fetch gate (`arch_speed-parallelism §3a`) then short-circuits the majority of work.
- Subscribe to the **delta/change feeds** the country files name (Bolagsverket/brreg/CVR/Companies House daily change files; `arch_fusion-confidence §4.3` references these but no component owns them) so the index stays fresh without re-pulling.
- The orchestrator must consult the bulk index in pre-flight; only the *residual* (contacts/email/phone the registries don't carry, plus the no-bulk markets IS/HU/IT/ES/PT/LU) ever reaches the scraper cascade.

### P0-2. LinkedIn coverage is a named goal but never designed — and the one place it appears violates ToS recklessly.
**Gap.** Goal #8 lists "every LinkedIn profile." `arch_oss-combo-matrix` row 10 says: "headless login once → export storage_state/cookies → replay all subsequent reads with curl_cffi carrying cookies … 1 login → 50 reads." That is automated authenticated LinkedIn scraping, which is the exact conduct enjoined in *hiQ v. LinkedIn* (post-remand) and a CFAA/ToS landmine that can burn the operating identity and trigger legal action. No arch file designs a *safe* LinkedIn lane (public DDG/Startpage/SearXNG SERP resolution, the approach the existing `contact-intel` / `company-contact-finder` skills already use), risk-budgets it, or even defines the `linkedin_urn` resolution path that `arch_fusion-confidence §1.2` assumes exists.
**Why it breaks goals.** #4 (self-correct/avoid blocks), #8 (coverage), and the platform's own compliance moat (`arch_fusion-confidence §0, §7`).
**Fix.** Replace row-10's "login + cookie replay" with a **public-surface LinkedIn resolution lane**: multi-engine SERP search (`site:linkedin.com/in`) → fuzzy-match name+company+title → store `linkedin_urn` with confidence, never authenticated crawl. Give LinkedIn its own **strict risk budget** (the `RPS["linkedin.com"]=0.2` in `arch_speed-parallelism §2c` exists but nothing enforces a global daily cap or ties it to identity burn). Document authenticated LinkedIn as an explicit out-of-scope/forbidden method in the compliance section, consistent with the honesty moat.

### P0-3. Redis is a textbook single point of failure for the entire platform.
**Gap.** Redis is simultaneously the broker, cache, Bloom store, rate-limit store, method-memory store, identity-burn store, and bandit-posterior store (`arch_speed-parallelism §2b` "one dependency does four jobs"; `arch_adaptive-engine §2.2, §5.2, §9`). There is no HA story, no persistence/restore plan, and no degraded-mode behavior. If Redis dies or flushes: the bandit forgets every learned method (re-pays the full OSS climb on every host), the Bloom filter resets (re-scrapes everything — directly violating the dedup speed win), every token bucket resets (thundering-herd into per-host blocks → mass identity burn), and circuit-breaker state is lost.
**Why it breaks goals.** #3, #4, #5, #6 all depend on this shared state surviving.
**Fix.** (a) Run Redis with AOF persistence + replica + Sentinel/Cluster (the doc already considers Redis Cluster for sharding in `§8` — extend it to HA). (b) Treat the bandit posteriors, method-memory, and source-reliability tables as **durable** state: periodically snapshot to Postgres/Fabric (the canonical store from `arch_fusion-confidence §8`) and reload on cold start, so learning survives a flush. (c) Define **degraded mode**: if Redis is unavailable, fall back to conservative fixed concurrency + cheapest-method-first rather than failing open into aggressive defaults.

### P0-4. Compliance contradiction: arch files say "scrape," country files say "criminal exposure / WAF-hostile / forbidden reuse."
**Gap.** The arch files frame every target as climbable by escalating tiers. The country files document hard legal stops the arch design never encodes:
- **DE Handelsregister:** 60 requests/hour cap, **§§303a/b StGB criminal exposure** for exceeding (`country_dach`). `arch_oss-combo-matrix`'s "escalate to Botasaurus/Sequentum" would walk straight into this.
- **ES CORPME `opendata.registradores.org` / `sede.registradores.org`:** WAF rejects bots; `country_southern` says "Do NOT scrape with a naive crawler." The bandit in `arch_adaptive-engine §2` would happily escalate to a browser and keep hammering.
- **BE KBO open data** and **DE directories:** contact fields present **but reuse for direct marketing is legally prohibited** (`country_benelux-fr`, `country_dach`). Nothing in the fusion/output layer encodes a per-source *reuse* restriction (distinct from per-country DNC).
- **NL KVK bulk is anonymised** (no name, no KVK number) — `arch_fusion-confidence` lists `NL_KVK` as a clean deterministic merge key, but the free source can't supply it.
**Why it breaks goals.** Compliance is the platform's stated moat (`arch_fusion-confidence §0, §7, §9`); these are landmines that can shut the whole operation down.
**Fix.** Add a **per-source policy table** (not just per-country) keyed `(source, country) → {rate_cap_hard, automated_access_forbidden, reuse_restriction, legal_note}`, loaded from the `country_*` files, that the router consults *before* selecting a method. Hard-cap DE Handelsregister at 60/hr and route it to OffeneRegister bulk instead; mark CORPME sede as `automated_access_forbidden` so the bandit never escalates a browser there (route to BOE BORME API instead); tag KBO/DE-directory contact fields with `reuse=no_direct_marketing` and have the output layer's `lawful_basis` filter enforce it. Make "a free bulk/API exists → never scrape" (stated in `country_benelux-fr §pipeline`) a **hard precondition in the router**, not advice.

### P0-5. Licensed-tool seat starvation has no global queue or overflow path — the scarce resource throttles the whole pipeline silently.
**Gap.** `arch_licensed-tools §7` models seats as per-backend `SeatPool` semaphores and a per-tick bin-packer, but seats are tiny (SF=4, Sequentum desktop=2, UiPath/Ranorex = a handful). At 50M scale the residual hard-target tail that *only* licensed tools can crack will vastly exceed seat throughput. The design says "Sequentum Cloud / UiPath JobsCount absorb spillover" but never defines the **overflow trigger**, the cost ceiling, or what happens when even Cloud is saturated — targets just silently wait or DLQ. There is also no **cross-backend global seat view**: SF, Sequentum, UiPath, Ranorex seat pools are independent, so the scheduler can't trade one scarce seat class against another.
**Why it breaks goals.** #2 (use every licensed tool maximally) and #3 (max speed) — a 6-seat ceiling is the platform's real throughput wall for hard targets, and it's unmanaged.
**Fix.** (a) A single **LicensedSeatBroker** with a global priority queue across all four tools; targets carry a `value`/`urgency` and the broker allocates the highest-value target to the best-fit *free* seat across all backends. (b) Define an explicit **Cloud-overflow policy**: when desktop seats are full AND backlog age > threshold AND target value > cost-of-cloud-call, spill to Sequentum Cloud / UiPath cloud robots with a per-day spend ceiling. (c) Surface seat saturation as a first-class metric that KEDA-style autoscaling and the `value` threshold both react to (raise the "is this target worth a seat" bar when seats are scarce).

## P1 — HIGH (a goal is partially met, or a real bottleneck/risk is unaddressed)

### P1-1. "Maximally parallel" is contradicted by Python `asyncio.Semaphore` internals and a serialized router.
**Gap.** `arch_adaptive-engine §4.2` resizes the AIMD window by poking `self._sem._value` (a CPython private) and the `acquire()` path holds a single `asyncio.Lock` around the token-bucket refill — serializing *every* request on that host through one lock. `MethodRouter._load`/`choose` hits Redis per host and caches in a per-worker dict with no invalidation, so fleet workers diverge. None of this is "maximally parallel"; it's a set of per-host serialization points.
**Fix.** Use a real async limiter (`aiolimiter`/`aiometer`, already in the dep list) instead of hand-rolled semaphore surgery; make the bandit posterior read a lock-free Redis sample (or `mabwiser` shared state) rather than a per-worker cache that silently goes stale; document the cache TTL/invalidation so all workers converge on a host's learned method.

### P1-2. The speculative "race + cancel" pattern wastes the resources it claims to save, and double-charges paid sources.
**Gap.** `arch_speed-parallelism §3b` and `arch_oss-combo-matrix §6` fire the scrape and the confirmer concurrently, then cancel the loser. But (a) `task.cancel()` on an in-flight `curl_cffi`/browser request does **not** free the socket/Chromium tab instantly — the proxy bandwidth and RAM are already largely spent; (b) racing **Apollo enrich** (a paid credit, `arch_speed-parallelism §3b` lists `apollo_enrich` in the race) against a free registry means you can **burn a credit you didn't need** if the registry would have won. The "hedged" variant in the same section fixes (a) partially but the headline `resolve_company` does not.
**Fix.** Make staggered hedging the default, not the parallel-everything race: fire free/cheap tier-0 first, and only launch the expensive/paid tier after a short deadline if cheap hasn't answered. Never put a **paid** source (Apollo) in the initial parallel wave — gate it behind the same cost-aware counter as paid proxies/CAPTCHA. This is also a direct cost bug, not just speed.

### P1-3. Vehicle coverage is structurally impossible as scoped, and the design doesn't say so honestly.
**Gap.** Goal #8 + `arch_fusion-confidence §1.2` model `vehicle.owner_uid`. The country files are unanimous: **owner-level vehicle data is closed in essentially every market** — SE (paid FTP), NO (no owner), FI/NL/CH/IT/ES (anonymised, no owner), DE/IE/PL/CZ/HU/FR/PT (restricted to authorities). Only Nordic reseller scrapes (biluppgifter, merinfo, Blocket) yield owner linkage, and only for SE-ish data. The arch presents `owner_uid` as a normal lawful-basis-gated field, masking that for 18 of 20 markets it is **irreducibly unavailable** by the platform's own legal rules.
**Fix.** Split vehicle into two honestly-scoped tiers: **(a) technical/fleet data** = broadly available free bulk (RDW NL, Traficom FI, ACI/MIT IT, ASTRA CH, DGT ES microdata, UK VES/MOT) — wire these as bulk sources per P0-1; **(b) owner linkage** = Nordic-only, reseller-scrape + `merinfo`/`biluppgifter`/`blocket` skills, explicitly flagged as a coverage gap elsewhere. Don't let the canonical model imply pan-European owner coverage that the compliance layer forbids.

### P1-4. No backpressure from the slow CPU/GPU parse pool to the fast fetch fleet → unbounded queue growth.
**Gap.** `arch_speed-parallelism §4c` correctly separates IO and CPU pools and hands raw bytes over the broker. But there is **no backpressure** the other way: if the parse/OCR/NER fleet (the slower stage) falls behind, the fetch fleet keeps producing and the `parse` Redis stream grows without bound until Redis OOMs — and the freshly-scraped HTML is the most memory-heavy payload. KEDA scales the parse pool on stream length (`§7a`) but scaling has latency and a ceiling (GPU pods are capped).
**Fix.** Add a **bounded parse queue with producer backpressure**: when the `parse` stream depth exceeds a high-water mark, the IO limiter's `acquire()` blocks (or the scheduler de-prioritizes new fetches) until it drains. This closes the loop so throughput is governed by the *slowest necessary* stage, not the fastest, preventing the documented "single most common throughput regression."

### P1-5. The bandit's non-stationarity decay can thrash, and cold-start on 50M new hosts is unmodeled.
**Gap.** `arch_adaptive-engine §2.2` decays Beta counts hourly toward the (1,1) prior (γ=0.98/hr). With sparse traffic per host (most of 50M hosts are hit a handful of times), the posterior is almost always near the prior, so Thompson sampling is effectively random and the "cheapest-method-first" intent is lost — every rarely-seen host re-explores expensive arms. Conversely a flapping WAF causes method thrash. There's also no **contextual prior**: a brand-new `.se` company site behind Cloudflare should inherit the learned policy for "`.se` + Cloudflare," not start from scratch.
**Fix.** Add a **hierarchical/contextual prior** (the `mabwiser` LinUCB path the doc already cites in `§2.1`): back off a host's sparse posterior to a cluster prior keyed on `(WAF vendor, TLD, page-type)` so a new host starts at the empirically-best method for its *class*, not the flat prior. Tune decay per-traffic-volume (slow decay for rarely-hit hosts). This is what makes "sense which method works per target" actually work at the long tail.

### P1-6. PDF/OCR is the documented financial-data path but has no scale lane, and `KB/bert` etc. NER models in country files aren't wired to the pipeline.
**Gap.** Financial filings for the **no-bulk markets** (IT/ES/PT visure, LU/IS per-doc, DE ~€1/doc) are PDF/XBRL retrieved per company — potentially millions of documents. `arch_oss-combo-matrix` Chain C handles OCR but `arch_speed-parallelism §8` budgets "1 GPU suffices" assuming only a small image fraction. More importantly, the country files specify the **correct per-language NER model per market** (KB/bert SE, FinBERT FI, camembert FR, flair-german, HuSpaCy HU, DaCy DK, etc.), but the arch pipeline hardcodes `en_core_web_md` / `xx_ent_wiki_sm`. Using the multilingual fallback everywhere throws away the accuracy the country research already found.
**Fix.** (a) Add a **language-routing NER dispatcher** that maps `document.lang → best model` using the exact model IDs in the `country_*` files, falling back to `xx_ent_wiki_sm` only when no dedicated model exists (IS, LB). (b) Re-budget OCR/XBRL for the no-bulk-financials tail and make XBRL parsing (structured, no OCR) the fast path — only scanned/PDF-only filings hit OCR.

### P1-7. iXBRL/XBRL financial data is treated as OCR, but it's structured — a missed free, fast, exact lane.
**Gap.** SE (weekly iXBRL bulk), UK (accounts XBRL), FR (INPI), BE (NBB XBRL/JSON/CSV), NL, FI, IT/DE (once purchased) all expose **machine-readable XBRL**. Chain C (`arch_oss-combo-matrix §7`) routes filings through `PyMuPDF → OCR`, which is wrong for XBRL: it's already structured financial facts with no OCR needed. This both wastes compute and loses precision (OCR'd numbers vs. tagged facts).
**Fix.** Add an **XBRL/iXBRL ingestion path** (e.g. `arelle`/`python-xbrl` or direct iXBRL parse) as the primary financials lane; OCR is only for legacy scanned PDFs. For SE specifically, the weekly iXBRL bulk is a *bulk source* (P0-1), not a per-company scrape at all.

## P2 — MEDIUM (correctness/robustness improvements; lower blast radius)

### P2-1. Email SMTP-RCPT probing at scale will get the sending IPs blacklisted.
`arch_fusion-confidence §3.6` and `arch_oss-combo-matrix` row 16 do throttled SMTP RCPT probes. At volume this trips Spamhaus/greylisting and burns the probe IPs. Fix: prefer MX + catch-all detection + cross-source agreement (already in the fusion model); reserve SMTP RCPT for premium/low-volume, from dedicated warmed IPs, never from scraping proxies.

### P2-2. FlareSolverr is a known throughput bottleneck and is on the critical CF path.
It's single-browser-per-request, slow (0.5–2 rps per `arch_adaptive-engine`), and frequently stale against current Cloudflare. The cascade leans on it (`arch_oss-combo-matrix §4`). Fix: prefer the `cf_clearance`-harvest-then-curl_cffi-replay path (already described) and camoufox over FlareSolverr; treat FlareSolverr as optional, pooled, and health-checked, not a required rung.

### P2-3. Free-proxy fabric health-checks against `httpbin.org` — a single external dependency and a weak liveness signal.
`arch_oss-combo-matrix §3` checks proxies via `httpbin.org/ip`. That endpoint is rate-limited/flaky and "can reach httpbin" ≠ "works against the target's WAF." Fix: health-check against a rotating set of *self-hosted* echo endpoints and, better, score proxies on **per-target** success (a proxy good for brreg may be burned on allabolag), feeding the same per-(domain,proxy) metrics store.

### P2-4. Calibration ground-truth sourcing is asserted but not sourced.
`arch_fusion-confidence §5.5` promises isotonic calibration "against ground truth (registry-confirmed fields, customer bounce/answer feedback, manual QA)." For a cold-start platform there is no bounce/answer feedback yet, and registry-confirmed fields only calibrate the fields registries carry (not emails/phones — the ones that actually decay). Fix: bootstrap calibration from the registry-confirmable subset, explicitly mark email/phone confidence as **uncalibrated until feedback accrues**, and don't publish a calibrated number for fields with no ground truth yet (consistent with the honesty moat).

### P2-5. No observability / metrics-store schema for the very feedback loops the design depends on.
Every adaptive behavior (bandit, AIMD, seat utility, source reliability) reads/writes a "metrics store" that is referenced but never schematized or given a durability/aggregation story. Without it, none of the self-correction is measurable or debuggable. Fix: define the metrics schema (`(backend|method, domain, country) → rolling success/latency/block/cost`), its store (Postgres/Fabric, not just Redis EWMA), and dashboards/alerts on block-rate spikes and seat saturation.

### P2-6. Identity factory has placeholder/incoherent fingerprint derivation.
`arch_adaptive-engine §5.2` hardcodes `v = ... if False else 131` and derives the browser family from `brands[-1]`, which is brittle and can desync the `impersonate` token from the actual UA (the exact incoherence the section warns against). Fix: take the browser+version from the browserforge fingerprint deterministically and map to the impersonate token; add a unit test asserting UA-major-version == impersonate-version for every generated identity.

### P2-7. DNC/suppression is applied only at output, not at collection — wasted work + larger exposure window.
`arch_fusion-confidence §7.4` filters suppressed contacts at the output layer. They're still scraped, stored, and processed. Fix: check national DNC (NIX/Robinson/Bloctel) and erasure tombstones at **intake** so suppressed/erased contacts are never fetched or enriched, reducing both wasted scrape budget and the personal-data retention surface.

## Cross-cutting observations

- **The country research is excellent and largely unexploited.** The single highest-leverage action is P0-1 + P0-4: turn the `country_*` files into a machine-readable **source registry + per-source policy table** that the orchestrator consults, converting prose into routing decisions. Most other gaps shrink once bulk-first + policy-aware routing exists.
- **Three of five required entity types (LinkedIn, vehicle-owner, person/contact) are the weakest.** Company data is well-served by registries; the platform's actual differentiation and risk both live in the people/LinkedIn/vehicle layers, which the arch files under-design relative to the company layer.
- **The honesty/compliance moat is asserted in `fusion-confidence` but undercut by the scrape-everything framing in `oss-combo-matrix`/`adaptive-engine`.** They need a shared, enforced policy gate so the two halves of the platform stop contradicting each other.
